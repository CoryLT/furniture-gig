## NEXT DIRECTION (DECIDED, not yet built) — make the PIECE the hub

Cory's call: **merge My Jobs into the Pipeline.** You don't think "I have a job," you
think "I have this dresser and it needs painting." So hiring becomes an action *on a
piece*, and FlipWork becomes the tool that writes the ad + tracks everything — using
**outside platforms (Craigslist / Facebook) for reach**, which kills the no-audience
problem for good.

**Target flow:** add a piece (photo + title) → on that piece tap **"Find help"** →
answer a few questions (what needs doing, pay, location, timeline, how to apply) →
app generates a **copy/paste AD** (saved + reopenable) to drop on Craigslist/FB. Plus
bring the **Books-app-style cost-entry layout** onto the piece (Cory loved that layout).

**Craigslist reality (researched 6/17):** a CL post is plain text — you CANNOT embed a
real clickable "Apply Now" button; it becomes words. So the ad ends with a chosen
**"how to apply"**: (1) reply via the platform, (2) text/call me, or (3) "Apply here:
<FlipWork job link>" (reuse the share link). Keep the link **optional** — CL flags
link-heavy/spammy posts, and the gigs/labor section is the lenient one (jobs section
often paid). One job per ad, single title, right category. **Not legal advice — skim
each site's rules.**

**Keep under the hood:** the existing **gig record** still powers the apply link, the
applicant list, and the pay→labor-expense link onto the piece (`inventory_pieces.source_gig_id`,
`piece_expenses` with `category='labor'`). Only the **front door** changes.

**Build order (agreed):**
1. **"Find help" → ad generator on a piece** — START HERE (testable same day). Questions
   → formatted ad + Copy button + chosen apply method; save it on the piece/job so it
   reopens.
2. **Books-style cost entry on the piece.**
3. **Retire the standalone "My Jobs" menu + cleanup** — LAST, once the new front door
   feels right.

Before step 1: peek at how a piece is stored + the add-piece screen so the button fits.
